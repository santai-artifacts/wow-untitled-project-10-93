## The Meaning of 42

In Douglas Adams' *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy*, 42 is famously declared the "Answer to the Ultimate Question of Life, the Universe, and Everything," computed over seven and a half million years by a supercomputer named Deep Thought. The joke, of course, is that no one actually knows what the question is — rendering the answer delightfully useless. Beyond the fiction, 42 has taken on a life of its own as a cultural symbol for the absurdity of seeking a single, tidy explanation for existence. It pops up in mathematics, computer science, and everyday coincidence with eerie frequency, which only deepens its mystique. At its core, 42 reminds us that the universe may not owe us a comprehensible answer — and that's somehow both humbling and freeing.
